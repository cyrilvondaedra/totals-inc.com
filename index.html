Understanding the Crisis

A Nation in the Grip of War

Following the February 2021 military coup, Myanmar descended into a humanitarian catastrophe. Armed conflict between the military junta and resistance forces has devastated communities, forced millions into displacement, and cut off access to food, medicine, and safe water across large swathes of the country.

Ethnic minorities — including the Karen, Kachin, Chin, Rohingya, and Shan peoples — bear the heaviest burden, fleeing air strikes, village burnings, and land-mine dangers into jungle encampments and over-crowded refugee settlements in Thailand, Bangladesh, and India.

Totals Inc. works directly with local community networks to reach the most vulnerable, cutting through aid bottlenecks to deliver life-saving support wherever it is needed most.

What We Do

Programs That Save & Rebuild Lives

Our holistic approach addresses immediate survival needs while building the foundation for long-term dignity and self-reliance.

🍚

Emergency Food Aid

Monthly food packs for displaced families in border camps and jungle settlements. We partner with local farmers to source and distribute nutritionally balanced rations.

⛺

Shelter & Protection

Emergency shelter kits, waterproof tarps, and semi-permanent transitional housing for newly displaced families — delivered within 72 hours of displacement.

🩺

Medical Care

Mobile health clinics, trauma care, maternal health services, and mental health counseling across camps in Thailand and communities inside Myanmar.

📚

Children's Education

Temporary learning centers, trained refugee teachers, and digital learning tools so that children in displacement are not robbed of their futures.

💧

Clean Water & Sanitation

Water purification systems, latrine construction, and hygiene kits for camps — reducing waterborne disease outbreaks that devastate crowded displacement settlements.

🤝

Livelihoods & Resilience

Vocational training, micro-grants for small businesses, and agricultural support to help refugees build economic independence in host communities.

Voices from the Ground

Stories of Survival & Hope

These are the lives behind the numbers.

We had to leave our village in the middle of the night. Everything we owned was left behind. The food and medicine from Totals Inc. kept my children alive in those first terrible weeks. I cannot put into words what that means.

— Ma Aye, 34, Karen State — now in Mae Sot, Thailand

My son was six when we fled. For two years he could not go to school. When the learning center opened in the camp, I watched him run inside — a light came back into his eyes that I had feared was gone forever.

— Daw Thuzar, 29, Chin State refugee, Bangladesh

The mobile clinic came when my mother was very sick. The doctors spent hours with her. In our situation, receiving that kind of care felt like a miracle. She is recovering now. We have hope again.

— Ko Zin Phyo, 22, Sagaing Region, displacement camp
